# About

I'm a PhD student at Purdue University, advised by Prof. Jing Gao and Prof. Xiaoqian Wang. Most of my recent work is on collaboration between heterogeneous LLM systems, at three layers of the stack: cross-family RL post-training, latent-space communication between heterogeneous VLM agents, and tokenizer transplantation in cross-family merging. I also work on LLM copyright compliance, spanning detection, agent-based defense, and behavioral unlearning. More recently, I have been revisiting graph models for low-latency, on-device LLM agents, where user activity is naturally structured rather than textual.
